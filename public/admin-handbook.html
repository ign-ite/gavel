Admin Review Standard

Gavel Admin Handbook

Admins are responsible for approving only listings that are accurate, reviewable, policy-compliant, and safe for buyers. Every decision must be traceable to the listing, media, and seller disclosures shown in the review queue.

Review the actual item, not the seller intent.
Reject with corrective notes, not vague warnings.
Escalate suspicious cases instead of guessing.

Approval Requirements

  1. Title, category, and price setup must match the actual product being sold.
  2. Images must clearly show the product, visible condition, and any relevant markings or defects.
  3. Video must be usable for moderation: stable enough to inspect, showing the actual item, and long enough to verify continuity.
  4. Description must disclose condition, included accessories, notable defects, and claims of authenticity or provenance.
  5. Listing must not appear stolen, counterfeit, prohibited, manipulated, or materially misleading.
If the evidence is incomplete, the correct action is rejection with a clear fix list, not approval based on assumption.

Reject When

  • Media is blurry, duplicated, rotated incorrectly, heavily filtered, or unrelated to the product.
  • Title or category is chosen to game discovery instead of accurately classifying the item.
  • Condition, authenticity, damage, missing parts, or provenance are omitted or contradicted by the media.
  • The seller makes unsupported value or authenticity claims.
  • The listing could create legal, fraud, safety, or reputational risk for the platform.

Conflict And Conduct Rules

  • Never approve your own listing or any listing where you have a financial or personal conflict.
  • Do not fast-track listings outside the queue for friends, repeat sellers, or internal pressure.
  • Do not rewrite the seller’s listing into a materially different product claim during moderation.
  • Use review notes professionally. Notes become part of the platform record.

Writing Review Notes

Use notes that explain exactly what must change. A useful note identifies the missing proof, the incorrect media, or the undisclosed issue.

  • Good: “Add a clear image of the serial plate and disclose the cracked bezel in the description.”
  • Bad: “Needs better details.”
  • Good: “Category should be Collectibles, not Electronics. Re-upload video in the correct orientation.”

Escalation Triggers

  • Potential counterfeit goods, mismatched ownership claims, or suspiciously altered media.
  • Repeated policy abuse from the same seller.
  • Threats, bribery attempts, or pressure to approve outside normal review.
  • Any case where the evidence suggests fraud, stolen goods, or platform abuse.

Escalate these cases to a super admin with a concise summary and the exact issue observed. Do not approve them while waiting for clarification.
